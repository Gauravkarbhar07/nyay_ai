=== BHARATIYA NYAYA SANHITA (BNS) 2023 ===

Section 101 - Murder:
Whoever causes the death of a person with the intention of causing death, or with the intention of causing such bodily injury as is likely to cause death, shall be punished with death, or imprisonment for life, and shall also be liable to fine. This replaces IPC Section 302.

Section 115 - Grievous Hurt:
Whoever voluntarily causes grievous hurt shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to seven years, and shall also be liable to fine. Grievous hurt includes permanent disfigurement, loss of limb or joint, fracture of bone.

Section 303 - Theft:
Whoever intending to take dishonestly any moveable property out of the possession of any person without that person's consent, moves that property, is said to commit theft. Punishment: imprisonment up to 3 years, or fine, or both.

Section 308 - Extortion:
Whoever intentionally puts any person in fear of any injury to that person, or to any other, and thereby dishonestly induces the person so put in fear to deliver to any person any property or valuable security, or anything signed or sealed which may be converted into a valuable security, commits extortion. Punishment: up to 3 years imprisonment.

Section 316 - Criminal Breach of Trust:
Whoever, being in any manner entrusted with property, or with any dominion over property, dishonestly misappropriates or converts to his own use that property, commits criminal breach of trust. Punishment: up to 7 years imprisonment.

Section 318 - Cheating and Fraud:
Whoever, by deceiving any person, fraudulently or dishonestly induces the person so deceived to deliver any property to any person, or to consent that any person shall retain any property, commits cheating. Punishment: up to 3 years, or fine, or both.

Section 351 - Assault:
Whoever makes any gesture, or any preparation intending or knowing it to be likely that such gesture or preparation will cause any person present to apprehend that he who makes that gesture or preparation is about to use criminal force to that person, is said to commit an assault.

Section 74 - Assault on Woman with Intent to Outrage Modesty:
Whoever assaults or uses criminal force to any woman, intending to outrage or knowing it to be likely that he will thereby outrage her modesty, shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which shall not be less than one year but which may extend to five years, and shall also be liable to fine.

Section 64 - Rape:
Whoever commits rape shall be punished with rigorous imprisonment for a term which shall not be less than ten years, but which may extend to imprisonment for life, and shall also be liable to fine. A woman can report rape at any police station regardless of where the incident occurred. The complainant's statement must be recorded by a female police officer.

Section 85 - Cruelty by Husband or His Relatives:
Whoever, being the husband or the relative of the husband of a woman, subjects such woman to cruelty shall be punished with imprisonment for a term which may extend to three years and shall also be liable to fine. Cruelty includes wilful conduct likely to drive a woman to suicide, or harassment for dowry.

Section 84 - Dowry Death:
Where the death of a woman is caused by any burns or bodily injury or occurs under suspicious circumstances within seven years of marriage, and it is shown that she was subjected to cruelty or harassment by her husband or relatives in connection with demands for dowry, such death shall be called dowry death. Punishment: minimum 7 years, maximum life imprisonment.

=== BHARATIYA NAGARIK SURAKSHA SANHITA (BNSS) 2023 ===

Section 37 - Right of Arrested Person:
Every person arrested has the right to be informed of the grounds of arrest. The person arrested shall be informed of his right to bail. Every person arrested shall be produced before a magistrate within 24 hours of arrest. No person shall be detained beyond 24 hours without magistrate's order.

Section 43 - Right to Consult Legal Practitioner:
Every person accused of an offence shall have the right to consult, and to be defended by, a legal practitioner of his choice. Legal aid shall be provided free of cost if the accused cannot afford a lawyer.

Section 173 - FIR Registration:
Every information relating to the commission of a cognizable offence shall be recorded by the officer in charge of a police station. The FIR must be registered free of charge. If police refuse to register FIR, the complainant can approach the Superintendent of Police or file a complaint before a Magistrate.

Section 176 - Investigation by Police:
Police have the power to investigate cognizable offences without magistrate's permission. During investigation, police can examine witnesses, collect evidence, and make arrests. The investigation must be completed within 60 days for serious offences.

Section 193 - Zero FIR:
A Zero FIR can be filed at any police station regardless of jurisdiction. The police station receiving the Zero FIR must transfer it to the appropriate jurisdiction police station within 15 days.

=== PREVENTION OF SEXUAL HARASSMENT AT WORKPLACE (POSH) ACT 2013 ===

Section 2 - Definition of Sexual Harassment:
Sexual harassment includes any unwelcome acts or behavior (whether directly or by implication) namely: physical contact and advances, demand or request for sexual favours, making sexually coloured remarks, showing pornography, any other unwelcome physical, verbal, or non-verbal conduct of sexual nature.

Section 4 - Internal Complaints Committee (ICC):
Every employer with 10 or more employees must constitute an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC). ICC must have a presiding officer who shall be a woman employed at a senior level. At least 50% members of ICC must be women. External member from NGO must be included.

Section 9 - Complaint Mechanism:
An aggrieved woman can file a written complaint to ICC within 3 months from the date of incident. If ICC is not constituted, the complainant can approach the Local Complaints Committee (LCC) at district level. Complaint can be filed even if the woman has left the organization.

Section 11 - Inquiry Process:
ICC must complete inquiry within 90 days. Both parties must be given opportunity to present their case. Conciliation can be attempted with the consent of the aggrieved woman. The proceedings of ICC are confidential.

Section 13 - Action on Inquiry Report:
If the allegation is proved, the employer must take action including: written apology, warning, reprimand or censure, withholding promotion or increment, termination from service, payment of compensation to the woman.

Section 14 - Punishment for False Complaint:
If the ICC finds the complaint to be false or malicious, it may recommend action against the woman who filed the complaint.

=== LABOUR LAWS ===

Minimum Wages Act 1948:
Every worker is entitled to minimum wages as fixed by the State Government. Minimum wages vary by industry and skill level. An employer paying below minimum wages commits an offence punishable with imprisonment up to 6 months or fine up to Rs. 500 or both. Workers can file complaint with Labour Commissioner.

Equal Remuneration Act 1976:
It is unlawful to pay unequal remuneration to men and women workers for same work or work of similar nature. If employer discriminates, worker can file complaint with Labour Authority. Penalty: imprisonment up to one month or fine up to Rs. 10,000 or both.

Child Labour (Prohibition and Regulation) Amendment Act 2016:
Employment of children below 14 years is completely prohibited. Children between 14-18 years cannot work in hazardous occupations. Penalty for employing child labour: imprisonment of 6 months to 2 years, or fine of Rs. 20,000 to Rs. 50,000.

Maternity Benefit Act 1961 (Amended 2017):
Every woman employee is entitled to maternity leave of 26 weeks for first two children. For third child onwards: 12 weeks. Leave can be taken 8 weeks before expected delivery. Employer cannot discharge or dismiss woman during maternity leave. Applicable to establishments with 10 or more employees.

Payment of Gratuity Act 1972:
Gratuity is payable to employees who have completed 5 years of continuous service. Gratuity = Last drawn salary × 15/26 × Number of years of service. Maximum gratuity limit is Rs. 20 lakh. Employer must pay gratuity within 30 days of it becoming payable.

Employees Provident Fund (EPF):
Employee and employer each contribute 12% of basic salary to EPF. Employee can withdraw EPF for marriage, education, home loan, medical emergency. Full withdrawal allowed on retirement or after 2 months of unemployment.

Workmen's Compensation Act 1923:
If a worker dies or gets injured during employment, compensation must be paid. For death: 50% of monthly wages × relevant factor based on age. For permanent total disablement: 60% of monthly wages × relevant factor. Claim must be filed within 2 years of accident.

Contract Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act 1970:
Contract workers must be provided basic amenities like drinking water, toilet facilities, canteen. They are entitled to same wages as regular workers doing same work. If contractor fails to pay wages, principal employer is responsible.

=== RIGHT TO INFORMATION (RTI) ACT 2005 ===

Section 6 - Filing RTI Application:
Any citizen can file RTI application to any Public Information Officer (PIO). Application can be written in Hindi, English, or official language of the area. Fee: Rs. 10 for Central Government. Some state governments have different fees. BPL card holders are exempt from paying fees.

Section 7 - Time Limit for Response:
PIO must respond within 30 days of receiving application. For information concerning life and liberty of a person: 48 hours. If PIO fails to respond, it is deemed refusal and First Appeal can be filed.

Section 19 - Appeal:
If unsatisfied with PIO response, First Appeal can be filed within 30 days to First Appellate Authority. Second Appeal can be filed before State/Central Information Commission within 90 days of First Appeal decision. CIC/SIC can impose penalty on PIO for delay or wrong information.

=== DOMESTIC VIOLENCE ACT 2005 ===

Section 3 - Definition of Domestic Violence:
Domestic violence includes: physical abuse (assault, beating, kicking), sexual abuse, verbal and emotional abuse (insults, humiliation, threats), economic abuse (not providing maintenance, denying access to money).

Section 12 - Application to Magistrate:
An aggrieved woman can directly approach Magistrate. Protection Officer assists woman in filing application. Application can be filed in court having jurisdiction where: the woman resides, the respondent resides, the domestic violence occurred.

Section 18 - Protection Order:
Magistrate can issue protection order prohibiting the respondent from committing domestic violence, entering workplace or school of aggrieved person, attempting to communicate with the aggrieved person, alienating assets.

Section 19 - Residence Order:
Aggrieved woman has the right to reside in shared household. Respondent cannot evict the woman from shared household without court order. Magistrate can direct respondent to provide alternative accommodation.

Section 20 - Monetary Relief:
Magistrate can direct respondent to pay monetary relief including: loss of earnings, medical expenses, maintenance for herself and children, loss caused by destruction of property.

=== LEGAL AID ===

National Legal Services Authority (NALSA):
Free legal aid is available to: persons earning less than Rs. 1 lakh per annum, women and children, persons belonging to SC/ST, victims of disasters, industrial workmen, persons in custody. Contact: NALSA Helpline 15100.

State Legal Services Authority (SLSA):
Every state has SLSA providing free legal services. District Legal Services Authority (DLSA) operates at district level. Lok Adalats provide quick dispute resolution. Legal aid clinics operate in courts and jails.

=== CONSUMER PROTECTION ACT 2019 ===

Section 2 - Definition of Consumer:
A consumer is any person who buys goods or avails services for personal use, not for resale. Online purchases are also covered under this Act. Consumers have the right to be protected against unfair trade practices and defective products.

Section 35 - District Consumer Commission:
Any consumer can file a complaint with the District Consumer Commission for disputes up to Rs. 50 lakh. State Consumer Commission handles disputes between Rs. 50 lakh and Rs. 2 crore. National Consumer Dispute Redressal Commission (NCDRC) handles disputes above Rs. 2 crore. Complaints can also be filed online at the National Consumer Helpline: 1800-11-4000.

Section 47 - Unfair Trade Practices:
Unfair trade practices include: making false claims about products, misleading advertisements, hoarding and black marketing, charging more than the MRP (Maximum Retail Price). Penalty for misleading advertisements: up to Rs. 10 lakh and up to 2 years imprisonment.

=== POCSO ACT 2012 (PROTECTION OF CHILDREN FROM SEXUAL OFFENCES) ===

Section 4 - Penetrative Sexual Assault:
Whoever commits penetrative sexual assault on a child shall be punished with rigorous imprisonment for not less than ten years, extendable to life imprisonment, and fine. Child means any person below the age of 18 years.

Section 19 - Mandatory Reporting:
Any person who has knowledge of an offence under POCSO MUST report it to the Special Juvenile Police Unit or local police. Failure to report is a punishable offence (up to 6 months imprisonment or fine). Medical professionals must also report.

Section 33 - Special Courts:
Special Courts shall be designated to try POCSO cases. Evidence of the child shall be recorded with special care. Child's identity shall not be disclosed. Child-friendly procedures must be followed during recording of statement.

=== CYBER CRIME AND IT ACT 2000 ===

Section 66 - Hacking / Data Theft:
If any person dishonestly or fraudulently does any act referred to in section 43 (damage to computer/data), he shall be punished with imprisonment up to 3 years or fine up to Rs. 5 lakh or both. This covers hacking, unauthorized access, data theft.

Section 66C - Identity Theft:
Whoever fraudulently or dishonestly makes use of the electronic signature, password, or any other unique identification feature of any other person shall be punished with imprisonment up to 3 years and fine up to Rs. 1 lakh.

Section 66D - Cheating by Impersonation on Computer:
Online fraud, phishing attacks, using someone's identity to cheat are punishable under Section 66D with up to 3 years imprisonment and fine up to Rs. 1 lakh.

Section 67 - Obscene Material Online:
Whoever publishes or transmits obscene material in electronic form shall be punished on first conviction with imprisonment up to 3 years and fine up to Rs. 5 lakh.

=== PROPERTY & LAND RIGHTS ===

Transfer of Property Act 1882 - Section 54 (Sale):
Sale of immoveable property worth more than Rs. 100 must be by a registered instrument. An unregistered sale deed has no legal validity. Buyer gets actual possession and ownership only after registered sale deed.

Registration Act 1908:
All documents relating to immoveable property (sale deed, gift deed, mortgage) must be compulsorily registered at the Sub-Registrar Office. Registration must be done within 4 months of execution. Stamp duty must be paid as per state government rates.

Land Acquisition Act (Right to Fair Compensation) 2013:
Government must pay compensation at 2–4 times the market value for rural land and 2 times the market value for urban land. Social impact assessment must be conducted before acquisition. Affected persons have the right to be heard.

=== MOTOR VEHICLES ACT 1988 (AMENDED 2019) ===

Section 134 - Accident Duty:
Driver involved in an accident must: stop the vehicle, render assistance to injured, take the injured to hospital, report to nearest police station within 24 hours. Failure to stop after accident: imprisonment up to 3 months and/or fine up to Rs. 10,000.

Section 161 - Hit-and-Run Compensation:
Victims of hit-and-run accidents are entitled to compensation without identifying the offending vehicle. Compensation: Rs. 2 lakh for grievous hurt, Rs. 5 lakh for death (under the Pradhan Mantri Motor Accident Compensation Scheme).

Section 212 - Traffic Offences and Fines (2019):
Driving without licence: Rs. 5,000 fine. Driving under influence (DUI): Rs. 10,000 fine and/or 6 months imprisonment. Over-speeding: Rs. 1,000-2,000 fine. Not wearing seatbelt/helmet: Rs. 1,000 fine. Talking on phone while driving: Rs. 5,000 fine.

=== DISABILITY RIGHTS ACT (RPWD) 2016 ===

Section 3 - Rights of Persons with Disabilities:
Every person with a benchmark disability (40% and above) has the right to: equality and non-discrimination, accessibility to public places and transport, equal education, equal employment opportunity. The Central Government must reserve 5% of seats in government housing and 4% in government jobs.

Section 24 - Equal Opportunity Policy:
Every employer having 20 or more employees must formulate an Equal Opportunity Policy. No person with disability can be denied appointment or promotion solely on basis of disability.

Section 92 - Offences:
Any person who intentionally insults or humiliates a person with disability shall be punished with imprisonment up to 6 months and/or fine of Rs. 10,000.

=== SENIOR CITIZEN WELFARE ===

Maintenance and Welfare of Parents and Senior Citizens Act 2007:
Children and relatives are legally bound to provide maintenance to parents and senior citizens. If they fail, the senior citizen can apply to the Maintenance Tribunal (SDM/District Collector). Tribunal can order maintenance up to Rs. 10,000 per month. Children who abandon parents can be imprisoned up to 3 months.

Section 23 - Transfer of Property by Senior Citizen:
If any senior citizen transfers property to a child/relative on the condition of care, and the child fails to provide care, the transfer can be declared void by the Tribunal.

वरिष्ठ नागरिक अधिकार – हिंदी:
बच्चों का कानूनी दायित्व है कि माता-पिता और वरिष्ठ नागरिकों का भरण-पोषण करें। यदि नहीं करते तो SDM/District Collector के पास Maintenance Tribunal में आवेदन करें। न्यायाधिकरण Rs. 10,000/माह तक का भरण-पोषण दिला सकता है। वृद्ध नागरिक हेल्पलाइन: 14567.

ज्येष्ठ नागरिक हक्क – मराठी:

=== ENVIRONMENT PROTECTION ===

Environment Protection Act 1986 - Section 15:
Any person who fails to comply with provisions of this Act shall be punished with imprisonment up to 5 years and/or fine up to Rs. 1 lakh. Continued contravention: additional fine up to Rs. 5,000 per day. NGT (National Green Tribunal) can be approached for environmental violations.

Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act 1974:
No person shall knowingly cause or permit any poisonous waste into any stream or well. Penalty: imprisonment from 1.5 to 6 years and fine. Public interest litigation can be filed in High Court for water pollution.

Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act 1981:
Burning waste, operating industrial units without pollution control devices is prohibited. State Pollution Control Board can order closure of polluting industries. Citizens can file complaints with State Pollution Control Board.

पर्यावरण अधिकार – हिंदी:
पर्यावरण प्रदूषण के खिलाफ National Green Tribunal (NGT) में याचिका दाखिल करें। दूषित जल स्रोत की शिकायत State Pollution Control Board को करें। अपने क्षेत्र में पर्यावरण उल्लंघन की रिपोर्ट करें। NGT हेल्पलाइन: 011-20521070.

पर्यावरण हक्क – मराठी:
पर्यावरण प्रदूषणाविरुद्ध National Green Tribunal (NGT) मध्ये याचिका दाखल करा. दूषित जलस्रोताची तक्रार महाराष्ट्र प्रदूषण नियंत्रण मंडळाकडे करा. NGT हेल्पलाइन: 011-20521070.


=== AADHAAR & DIGITAL RIGHTS ===

Aadhaar Act 2016 - Section 29:
Aadhaar number shall not be published, displayed, or posted publicly. No body corporate shall use Aadhaar for authentication except as permitted by the Act. Misuse of Aadhaar data is punishable by imprisonment up to 3 years and/or fine up to Rs. 10,000.

Data Protection:
Your personal data cannot be shared without your consent under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023. You have the right to access, correct, and erase your personal data. Data fiduciaries must notify you in case of data breach. Grievance Officer must respond within reasonable time.

आधार और डिजिटल अधिकार – हिंदी:

=== ANTI-CORRUPTION LAWS ===

Prevention of Corruption Act 1988 (Amended 2018):
Any public servant who demands, accepts, or obtains any gratification (bribe) other than legal remuneration commits an offence. Punishment: imprisonment from 3 to 7 years and fine. Bribe giver (if coerced) is protected if they report within 7 days. Anti-corruption helpline: 1064 (CBI) or Lokpal.

Section 17A - Prior Sanction for Investigation:
No investigation of a public servant above a certain level without prior sanction of the competent authority.

भ्रष्टाचार विरोधी कानून – हिंदी:
